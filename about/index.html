About Me

I'm Randy Walker — a technology entrepreneur, cloud strategist, and software developer based in Austin, Texas. I've spent over two decades helping organizations use technology to grow, transform, and compete.

I'm the owner of SK Meridian LLC, a technology company based in Austin focused on software development, cloud infrastructure, and platform engineering. Previously, I served as President of Harvest Data Corp, where I led enterprise data strategy and cloud transformation engagements for clients across retail, logistics, and financial services.

I was recognized as a Microsoft Most Valuable Professional (MVP) — an award given by Microsoft to independent technology experts who contribute to the developer community through speaking, writing, and open knowledge sharing. I was also an ASP Insider, part of a small group of external advisors who worked directly with Microsoft's ASP.NET product team.

Earlier in my career I was deeply involved in the .NET developer community — founding and leading user groups in Northwest Arkansas, speaking at conferences like TechEd, devLink, and TechFest, and serving on the INETA (International .NET Association) Board of Directors. I was recognized as an INETA Community Champion for that work.

What I Write About

This blog has been running since 2007, which makes it something of a time capsule of the Microsoft developer ecosystem across two decades. You'll find posts ranging from early Visual Studio and SQL Server tips to Azure, Power BI, and cloud DevOps — plus the occasional take on leadership, career, and technology strategy.

I write about what I'm working on, thinking about, or running into — with enough specificity to be useful and enough candor to be honest.
